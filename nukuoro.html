The Nukuoro Atoll

Return To Home Page

  • Including the lagoon, the area of the Nukuoro atoll is 40 km/15 miles squared.
  • The first inhabitants of the atoll consisted of man and crew of six who came from the nearby island of Tokelau.
  • The residents of the atoll today number roughly 372 strong and all speak the Polynesian language of Nukuoro.
  • The atoll was first encountered by Europeans in the 19th century when Spanish naval officer Juan Bautista Monteverde spotted it while on a voyage.
  • An irregularly scheduled passenger boat is the one of the only forms of contact that comes to this remote atoll.
